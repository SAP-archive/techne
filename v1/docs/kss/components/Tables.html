Use pagination components in conjunction with Tables (recommended) or Cards (if needed) to allow for more efficient performance and quick access to specific records in the overall data set.

15.1 Usage Guidelines

techne recommends displaying up to three pages in the pagination: the current page; the previous page and the next page, as well as arrows to go to the first page (<<) or last page (>>). Display pagination at the top and bottom of the data set. It is also recommended that the number of records is displayed at the top of the page (see Patterns & Layouts), not just the total number displayed on the single page.

When 3 pages or less, do not display arrows:


  • 1
  • 2
  • 3

When more than three pages, display arrows:


  • «
  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • »

On the first page: first number is active, « is disabled


  • «
  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • »
  • «
  • 2
  • 3
  • 4
  • »

On the middle page: middle number is always active


  • «
  • 3
  • 4
  • 5
  • »
  • «
  • 3
  • 4
  • 5
  • »

On the last page: last number is active, » is disabled
